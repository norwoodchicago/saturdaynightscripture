The levels of the christian life

Feeling like ranking up? Here is your cheat guide.

Why

Debating whether defining the 100 levels of the Christian life is biblical or theological is as much fun as arguing about what the 100 levels are.

The last three generations of christians have not faired well in a well-off liberal western capitalistic society. On the order of 95%, a christian generation raised apostate children that is raising their children as pagans. A level 5 christian needs a wake up call, and this list is it.

The bible is full of levels as binaries. For example, one person builds their house on sand and another builds their house on a rock. Jesus promises his disciples houses, as does Paul, leaving those without the promise one step below. Part of the trick is to string all of these together. It's a giant exercise in salvation math.

Creating a unified ranking system is hard to do because of the different paths people take to holiness. Think Martha versus Mary. On a practical note, those who leave everything and join a religious order don't need my list. I'm narrowing this list to the situation of the average American.

The most difficult challenge I encountered is to define a level one Christian. What does it mean to be a level one Christian? Does it buy salvation? I'm inspired by Paul's letters to the early churches where he outlines the behavior of believers, including accepting every false teaching, anger, infighting, judging others, and having drunken orgies. Level one has to be a low bar or we wouldn't have a church at all. That's where I'm going to start.

Level's 0 to 9

Staring with the ministry of Jesus himself, observers have critisized christians as rotten, sinful people. These critics were correct then and they are correct now.

At Level 1 a Christian gets out of bed, checks his phone, makes some coffee, and then decides that God will be his God instesd of all the alternative gods.

Level 2 At least once during the day, the person prays to God for help on some personal matter for himself or a family member, but mostly for himself.

Level 3 Mostly attends weekly services, and is not distracted during at least half of the service.

Level 4 Picks up a book written by the church fathers or a saint. Reads it.

Level 100

To get to Level 100, a person is specifically chosen to be the father of Jesus on earth and protector of his mother. Must remain silent through all four gospels.

Level 1,000,000

To earn level one million, a person is chosen by God to bring his son to all of humanity. Additional requirements include agreeing to all of God's terms before these are announced, accepting suffering, telling people to do whatever her son says to do, standing before the cross while her son is tortured to death, and then holding him when he is taken from the cross even though everyone else deserts him. Finally, this person will pray with his disciples leading up to the birth of the church.
