The two definitions

The pagan definition for wisdom is the collective guidance learned over the centuries by various cultures that can be used to culturely steer a person in their decision making on topics that require hundreds of years of experience to determine which option has the best outcome.

The catholic definition of wisdom is this: God is giving you one day to live and will help and support you this day if you make him your God and stay focused on what is right according to his Church. Tomorrow, you will get a different day. Repeat until you die.

Catholic wisdom is hard. The biggest problem is anxiously waiting all day for God to fix your problems. For problems that take more than a day to fix, it gets worse each day. "If only I could win the lotto", you think, "I could fix my problems with money and wouldn't need God". Later you learn that you're encouraged to give all that you have to the poor and true joy is in suffering.

The quest for a replacement god is a constant in all of human history. Replacement gods cause mass suffering, famine and death, but only if they can prevent the real God from helping. Thus the battle between the Catholic church and human wisdom.

If a person makes a statement that needs a thorough logical proof or scientific research to determine it's veracity, but everyone jumps on it waving flags like a parade float, then it's the world's wisdom and is wrong. Other ways to identify worldly wisdom: it lumps people into groups, it contradicts anything the Catholic church teaches, it makes groups or organizations into enemies, it espouses or supports violence.

Once you have identified a worldly wisdom candidate, it is much harder to call out lies. When the mob is holding a witch underwater to see if she is a witch or not, it's a little late to point out that witches don't exist.
