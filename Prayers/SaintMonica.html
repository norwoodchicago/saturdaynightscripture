Saint Monica, you were once the mournful mother of a prodigal son. Your faithfulness to prayer brought you and your son so close to God that you are now with him in eternity. You threw yourself on the mercy of God, putting all of your needs, worries, doubts and anxieties in his hands. Through sorrow and pain, you constantly devoted yourself to God. Pray for me, that I might join you in such a deep faith in God's goodness and trust in God's mercy.

That whole time you had to deal with an angry, critical mother-in-law and an alcoholic, unfaithful husband.

You showed up in Rome a rural, uneducated girl. You maintained your faith and your charitable heart.

You had three children and made their Christian education a priority amidst a pagan culture that didn't respect your beliefs.

As a parent and a Christian, you screwed up in numerous ways. When Augustine was 17, you kicked him out of the house for not having the right beliefs.

In desperation you turned to the church for help, like the nagging widow to the corrupt judge.

By age 18 Augustine had made just about every type of bad decision and sin. All those prayers for conversion - and he joined a cult. You didn't give up.

Somewhere between you driving away Augustine's parnter and beloved son, not the most Christian act in the world, and him actually converting, you yourself become a model Christian.

If the prayer of conversion requires my own conversion to be heard, then pray for me that I turn from anything that blocks my path to God so that I become a great saint like you.

Monica pray for us.
