The sad rich man

A unique encounter that doesn't end well

Those he meets

Jesus meets many people in the gospels. In all but one case, he heals and forgives.

Matthew 12:15, "Many people followed him, and he cured them all." Matthew 4:23, "He went around all of Galilee, teaching in their synagogues, proclaiming the gospel of the kingdom, and curing every disease and illness among the people."

There were a few groups who weren't happy to see Jesus, including the Pharasees, the Samaritans who lost their pig herd, and the Romans who ignored him. Some weren't even looking for Jesus, such as the woman at the well or the man in the tombs, and Jesus changed their lives.

Except the rich man

In Matthew 19, a rich man approaches Jesus and said, “Teacher, what good must I do to gain eternal life?” After confirming the commandments, the man presses Jesus, and Jesus replies that he must give away all he owns to the poor and follow Jesus. "When the young man heard this statement, he went away sad, for he had many possessions."

What makes this encounter unique is that the rich man didn't want healing or forgiveness, he just wanted to be ratified as good by Jesus.

The bottom line

Jesus doesn't accept anyone as they are. He accepts them, but the 'as they are part' goes into a dumpster.
