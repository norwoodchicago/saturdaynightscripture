Two different approaches

Two people went out to plant

One person was a Spanish speaker and one person was Scottish.

The Spanish speaker planted the seeds and watered it. He stood over the seeds, gently moved the dirt, watered some more, and began to fertalize the seeds. Like a mother, he smoothed the dirt over and waited. Then he watered some more.

The Scottish speaker left for a minute and came back with a box. He stepped up onto the box and yelled out to the people in the field, "Listen up! Everybody find some seeds and plant them. I'm not doing you're work for you!"

These are two real people

When a Spanish person from any Spanish speaking country wants to say something, he puts a lot of effort and care into the discussion. His genuine intent is to help you understand what he has to say. For each sentence delivered by a normal speaker, a Spanish speaking speaker will deliver 5. If you ask any questions, he will start over and tell you all of the things that he already told you. I call this the Patriarchal talking approach.

An English speaking person of a specific cultural tradition within the British Isles will provide one dry quip and expect you to figure it out for yourself. I call this the Industrial Revolution talking approach.

This section of the website is going to be a mix, but mostly Scottish.
